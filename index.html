Xylo Orr Summer Reading Part 2



Piece of Artistic Expression

The story “Nothing but the Truth” by Avi is about a student named Philip Malloy who was supposedly suspended for humming "The Star Spangled Banner" when his teacher, Ms. Margaret Narwin, repeatedly told him to be quiet. When Philip went to plead his case to others, he argued that it was a free country and he was just being patriotic, so others were shocked and horrified that Philip got suspended. However, what they don't understand is that Philip got suspended for repeatedly disobeying Ms. Narwin, not for being patriotic. Either way, the media got involved and the story that was run was Philip's version, not the teacher's version. There was an outpour of negative reactions toward the school officials and a lot of support for Philip. The story show's how one lie can impact the lives of many others. Ms. Narwin felt the need to resign from her teaching position, Harrison lost the funding it desperately needed, and Philip had to transfer to another school. His lie unravels at the end of the book when at Washington Academy, his new school, he is asked to sing the national anthem. Philip begins to cry and finally reveals the truth, "I don't know the words."

For my artistic piece I created a Graphic Interchange Format or GIF in photoshop to represent the theme of the story. The GIF shows Philip Malloy running away from Harrison and toward the American flag. However, there is also a fire behind Philip. The fire represents Philip's own destruction/childish ways and alludes to the popular childhood phrase, "Liar, liar, pants on fire." Even though Philip is running (track is important to him) toward the American flag (representing his apparent singing "The Star Spangled Banner"), he will never get there because he never sang it (the fire), he doesn't even know the words. He lied, and he's running away from the truth that lies with the school officials at Harrison (the school building).
